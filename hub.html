About the Network

StateImpact is a collaboration between NPR and member stations in eight pilot states to provide broadcast and online coverage of how public policy affects people’s lives. The project will launch one new site in each state that focuses around a policy issue of importance determined by member stations. The vision is to serve readers and listeners as a rich source of information on-air and online about how government decisions intersect with local communities.

The network comprises coverage of four initial policy areas: budgets, education, energy and the economy. The initial states participating in the project are Florida, Indiana, Idaho, New Hampshire, Ohio, Oklahoma, Pennsylvania and Texas. Pilot states were chosen based on member stations’ commitment to collaboration and issue-focused coverage within their states.

To facilitate our coverage goals, each state has committed to hire two multi-platform journalists, and a team at NPR is coordinating the project at a network level. StateImpact sites feature enterprise reporting, curation of outside content and original data projects — databases and data-driven interactives — aimed at helping make government information more accessible and educational. Across both platforms, expect StateImpact content to focus on providing context, analysis and connections that help audiences better understand the decisions that affect their lives.
